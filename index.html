SIR BAUDELAIRE: “SIR BAUDELAIRE,” the intro to Tyler, The Creator’s seventh studio album, introduces us to Tyler Baudelaire, the character Tyler assumes on CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST. Both Tyler himself and the album’s narrator, DJ Drama, refer to Tyler by the name throughout the record.

CORSO: “CORSO” is the introduction to the plot that we will later go through as the album progresses. Tyler has done this on multiple occasion in his discography such as “WOLF” from Wolf, in which we are introduced rather abruptly to the characters whose story will play out over the course of the album.“CORSO” is the introduction to the plot that we will later go through as the album progresses. Tyler has done this on multiple occasion in his discography such as “WOLF” from Wolf, in which we are introduced rather abruptly to the characters whose story will play out over the course of the album.

LEMONHEAD: The track features Tyler, 42 Dugg, and Frank Ocean showing their bravado by flaunting one another’s opulence and expensive possessions.

WUSYANAME: “WUSYANAME” portrays Tyler, The Creator falling madly in love with a woman he just met, shortly before finding out that she's taken.

LUMBERJACK: Tyler boasts his material wealth and addresses his haters over an abrasive beat that contrasts much of his more melodic production. The song’s instrumental heavily samples the August 1994 Gravediggaz cut “2 Cups of Blood.”

HOT WIND BLOWS: The chorus references the harsh wind conditions, but they remain unfazed and enjoy the view of the mountains from the lake

MASSA: On “MASSA” Tyler calls back to the era of slavery in the United States which took place from its founding in 1776 until 1865.

RUNITUP: In “RUNITUP,” Tyler is discussing the common theme of CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST–success–in light of the past situations that preceded it.

MANIFESTO: Throughout the song, he raps about his controversial past, how he refuses to conform to the standards other people want to hold him against, and how social media platforms like Twitter have changed to become a hub for social activism. In a way this is Tyler, The Creator’s response to cancel culture.

SWEET/I THOUGHT YOU WANTED TO DANCE: The feeling of falling head over heels for someone, they play along, but in the end they don't feel the same and cut it off.

MOMMA TALK: This song is exactly as the title suggests – a voice memo of Tyler, The Creator's mother talking.

RISE: "RISE!" by Tyler, The Creator is a powerful anthem about overcoming obstacles and rising to the top amidst adversity

BLESSED: Tyler being grateful for his success and blessings.

JUGGERNAUT: Tyler, The Creator is joined by Lil Uzi Vert and Pharrell Williams on “JUGGERNAUT,” a stone-cold banger that serves as track fourteen of his album CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST. It features continued contributions from DJ Drama, whose vocals are scattered throughout the album.

WILLSHIRE: Recorded in a single take, “WILSHIRE” sees Tyler tell the story of a failed relationship—from the point of him and his partner first laying eyes upon each other to their eventual breakup.

SAFARI: The lyrics convey a sense of wanderlust and adventure, with Tyler boasting about his extravagant lifestyle, luxury cars, and sharp suits.

EVERYTHING MUST GO: It serves as an interlude from the old songs, to the newer songs.

STUNTMAN: The song touches upon themes of jealousy and envy as some people try to join in on their success.

WHAT A DAY: Tyler reflects on the constant demands on his time and energy, feeling overwhelmed and overworked.

WHARF TALK: The song finds Tyler in love with a woman he does everything for. However, she doesn't want to commit to being with him for the rest of her life.

DOGTOOTH: "Dogtooth" is a song about Tyler's lust for a girl, his sense of superiority, and a warning to his rivals that they don't measure up. He's not shy about his wealth and ambition, bragging about buying his neighbor's house and turning it into a yard. Tyler's all about loyalty to his people too. He mentions his grandma Lynda, whom he loved and lost in 2013, and says that if you don't know her name, you're not close to him.

HEAVEN TO ME: Explores the concept of personal heaven, and how it can change over time.

BOYFRIEND, GIRLFRIEND: A song that talks about unrequited love and a desire for something deeper.

SORRY NOT SORRY: He is shown to be “fighting” the other personas like ones that represent ego and greed in the music video trying to apologize for the mistakes they made.
